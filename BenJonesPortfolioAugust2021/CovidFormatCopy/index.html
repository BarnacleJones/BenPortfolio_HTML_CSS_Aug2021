  • Group 4: age 60 - 64

Bay of Plenty is currently vaccinating the groups listed above, as well as all Māori and Pacific kaumātua and their whānau.
Once you’ve received your invitation or when your age band is open, go to BookMyVaccine.nz to book your appointment. You can also use the booking button below.
If you’re unable to book online, you can ring the COVID-19 Vaccination Healthline on 0800 28 29 26 and we’ll make the booking for you and answer any questions.

Getting a COVID-19 vaccine is an important step you can take to protect yourself, your kaumātua and whānau from the effects of the virus. For more information on the COVID-19 vaccines and eligibility visit Ministry of Health - COVID-19 vaccines
If you are getting a flu, MMR or other vaccine, it will affect timing of the COVID-19 vaccination. Please discuss with your health provider.

Assistance

  • NZ sign language interpreter
  • Support to make decisions
  • Assistance to move around
  • Quiet, low sensory environment
  • More space to move around

Please note - Our 87 First Avenue vaccination centre is unsuitable for wheelchair access or for those unable to use the stairs. Please book into our Trustpower Baypark vaccination centre instead. Trustpower Baypark provides space to move around and is fully wheelchair accessible.


For both vaccination centres:


A NZ sign language interpreter can be booked in advance by emailing ask. covidvax@bopdhb.govt.nz.


Our vaccination team are trained to support you to make decisions and we have flow coordinators at each site who can provide assistance to move ardoun, as and when required.


Trustpower Baypark provides space to move around and is fully wheelchair accessible.
